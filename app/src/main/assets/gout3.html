Complications of allopathic treatment

  • The treatment of acute gout allopathically blocks the inflammatory response with Colchicine, NSAIDs, and intraarticular steroids. 

  • When prescribing NSAIDs to the elderly, extreme caution should be exercised. 

  • Peptic ulcer disease, renal failure, uncontrolled hypertension, and cardiac failure are not recommended for patients taking these medications. 

  • Patients with gastrointestinal disorders may have difficulty taking Colchicine, as it can reach toxic levels.

  • The drugs may also damage the bone marrow and the kidneys in patients with renal or hepatic insufficiency. 

  • Intravenous Colchicine can result in myelosuppression, hepatic necrosis, seizures, renal failure, and death. 

  • Side effects of corticosteroids include glucose intolerance, electrolyte shifts, increased susceptibility to infection and hypertension.


Allopathic drugs that increase urate

  • A decrease in urate excretion by the kidneys and diuretics and aspirin to control hypertension and impaired renal function are important risk factors among the elderly. 

  • There were six patients with high blood pressure, two of whom took antihypertensive medications. 

  • Taking such drugs worsens gout symptoms, and alternative antihypertensive treatments should be considered for these patients.
